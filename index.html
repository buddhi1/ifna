Our Mission

The International Federation of Nonlinear Analysts (IFNA) is a not-for-profit educational and research oriented organization (society), that was founded more than 25 years ago, with the ambitious goal of assisting in changing our world one topic at a time. IFNA is globally an interdisciplinary professional organization which seeks to promote the understanding of related complex nonlinear problems and approaches to solutions from all disciplines using the art of mathematical sciences.

IFNA researchers from all fields of study from across the GLOBE seek solutions to real world problems facing our society in this age of information. There has been a growing need for this professional organization to assist in increased understanding of our Global Society. With more than 125 countries including developed, developing and undeveloped countries, IFNA serves as a global network where instant communication allows for assistance to be made Anywhere, Anytime, Anyplace.

Our globalization and technological evolution is becoming more complex, highly interactive and interdependent. Furthermore, the 21st century advances in technology have increasingly generated and used large, complex, and diverse datasets (BIG DATA). Such advancements in technology have engendered new problems that are different in nature and more global in scope. For example, now there are many serious problems of worldwide consequences, in GLOBAL WARMING, GLOBAL ECONOMY, GLOBAL HEALTH, GLOBAL ENERGY, among others. The better understanding of such complex problems requires the joint efforts of researchers from all disciplines including chemical, biological, ecological, economic, engineering, environmental, medical, physical, and sociological sciences, among others. With Mathematical and Statistical Sciences as a conceptual language and tool, we can address all global problems in all aspects of theory, methods and applications.

We at “IFNA WORLD” focus on “The Circles of Sustainability” as so clearly defined by the United Nations

Objectives

  • Create opportunities for the exchange of ideas among the scholars and bridge the cultural boundaries.
  • Help researchers from all fields of study across the globe to resolve current real world problems.
  • Encourage undergraduate and graduate exchange programs.
  • Encourage and assist educational activities in developed and undeveloped countries.
  • Assist in the creation of database that is essential for research and decision making.
  • Provide financial aid assistance international activities in education and research.
  • Use 21st century technology to develop partnership among researchers, scientists, and agencies around the globe that work on complex problems.
  • Build problem solving groups among the scholars around the globe.
  • Promote global centers for their innovative and cooperative solutions to the global real problems.
  • Host international conferences and other academic activities to enhance the education in the developing countries.
  • Establish communication between global centers using social media, web-based content, and traditional media.
  • Enhance the quality of life through research and development.

Research Areas

  • Economy / Finance
    • Analysis and modeling of behavioral, ecological, and energy economics.
    • Development of economic models for industrial competitive analysis among countries.
    • Game theory approach to economic problems with competitive interactions.
    • Economic understanding of regional effects: city, state, country.
    • Statistical analysis and modeling in identifying and ranking the risk factors of the various financial business segments.
    • Economic and financial models that are essential in formulating policies for controlling global warming problems.
    • Utilizing extreme value theory, non-parametric methodology and Bayesian analysis to address economic and financial systems.
    • The development of quantitative and qualitative global research on digital economy.
  • Energy
    • Analysis and modeling to better understand the effectiveness of renewable energy sources: wind, solar, and hydropower.
    • Evaluation modeling of the energy systems; availability, efficiency, and reliability.
    • Perform feasibility studies to identify the appropriate energy system regionally around the globe.
    • Understanding economical and environmental impacts of various energy sources.
    • Development and analysis of energy risk management models.
    • Analysis and modeling of LNG & PG production and storage.
    • Renewable energy sources in smart grids.
    • Communication technologies into grids and networks.
    • Cyber/physical reliability of energy systems.
  • Environment
    • Analysis and modeling including Bayesian, non-parametric and extreme value theory to better understand climate change.
    • Understanding “Global Warming.” Carbon-dioxide in the atmosphere interacting with atmospheric temperature.
    • Identifying the risk factors that cause Global Warming among different countries in the world.
    • Developing more accurate prediction methods for natural disasters such as hurricanes, tornados, and earthquakes.
    • Analysis and modeling to better understand the impact of industry on the environment and help create environmental policies.
    • Environmental impact of different energy sources.
    • Understanding environmental health issues – water and air quality, different energy sources, among others.
    • Nanotechnology and environmental nuclear issues.
    • Waste and toxic effects on the environment.
    • Economic impact on legal environmental policies on a regional basis, city, state, country.
  • Health
    • Analysis and modeling including Bayesian, non-parametric and extreme value theory to better understand the different types of diseases such as cancer and heart disease.
    • Effective evaluation and ranking of different treatments for health problems.
    • Identifying regional (city/state/country) effects on all health problems.
    • Develop cost effective management of health care systems.
    • Modeling and analysis of occupational and environmental health.
    • Studying the effects of smoking, passive smoking, nutrition, obesity and physical activities on individual health.
  • Global Cyber Security

Prof. Chris P. Tsokos, Ph.D.

President

  • Email: chris.tsokos@ifnaworld.org
  • Home

Dr. Tsokos is Distinguished University Professor of Mathematics and Statistics at the University of South Florida. He obtained his undergraduate degree in Engineering Sciences and his Master of Science in Mathematics from the University of Rhode Island and his Doctoral Degree in Probability and Statistics form the University of Connecticut. Dr. Tsokos is the author of more than 300 publications in academic journals. He has writtne 15 textbooks and research monographs and more than 40 technical publications. He served as Doctoral Advisor for more than 40 students from twelve different countries. He has served and is currently serving in more than 12 academic international journals. Dr. Tsokos has lectured in several countries with extensive invited lecture tours in the P.R. of China, Turkey, the Russian Academy of Science, and India among others. He has been an advisor and consultant for NASA, NSF, NIH, ONR, US Air Force, US Army, EPA, ACS, as well as several Fortune 500 companies. He is a member of several professional societies, which include a Fellow of the American Statistical Association and the International Statistical Institute, among others. He is the recipient of several distinguished honors and awards for his professional, educational and humanitarian accomplishments.

Bong-Jin Choi, Ph.D.

Vice President and Director of IFNA Journals and Publications

  • Email: bongjin.choi@ndsu.edu

Bong-Jin Choi was born in Busan, South Korea. He received the B.S. degrees in Computer Science from Kyungnam University, South Korea and received the Master and Ph.D. degree in Statistics in University of South Florida, Tampa in 2014. After earning Ph. D., he joined the UNC Comprehensive Cancer Center, University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill as big health care data manager. He joined Department of Statistics and Department of Public Health in North Dakota State University as an assistant professor in 2018. He has been teaching several courses for under-graduated and graduated students such as Applied Regression Models, Introductory Biostatistics, Biostatistics, and Survival Analysis. He has also supervised several M.S. and Ph.D. thesis. His current research interests include Machine Learning and Big Health Care Data Analysis. He has published more than 10 scientific papers and he is serving Managing Editor in Global Journal of Mathematical Sciences and Director of Journals in International Federation of Nonlinear Analysts.

Yong Xu, Ph.D.

Vice President

  • Email: yongxu@ferris.edu

Dr. Xu is an associate professor in Data mining at Ferris State University since fall 2017. He is founder of west Michigan Artificial Intelligence research lab. Dr. Xu got his Ph.D. degree from University of South Florida in 2011. He then joined Radford University in Virginia as a tenure-track assistant professor in 2011. He taught a variety of undergraduate classes in data analysis since 2011. He is interested in innovative teaching methods. He took numerous training in online class, flipped class, student-centered learning. He designed and created several classes including R class, SAS, and design of experiment class. He proposed and taught several new classes at Ferris State university including: Python, SQL, and Tableau classes. His research interest focuses on Machine learning, Big data, Business data, Environment data, and health data analysis. He had done some research related with big data, time series and statistical models. He had worked for Oak Ridge National Laboratory, American Cancer Society and H. Lee Moffitt Cancer Center. He had published more than a dozen research papers and made more than 20 professional presentations. He had applied for ten research grants (total more than 10 million dollars) and got four of them funded. In 2020, he had been awarded $339,000 research grant. He had made numerous consultations works for other researchers in both academic and industry (total 22). He is the Editor for Open Journal of Applied Sciences (OJAppS) and reviewer for seven peer reviewed journals.

Prof. Rebecca Wooten, Ph.D.

Secretary

  • Email: rwooten@ifnaworld.org
  • Home

Dr. Wooten is president of The Pedagogue, a Private Educational and Research Company that serves the public needs. She reived her Bachelors and Masters Degree in Mathematics and Doctoral Degree in Statistics from the University of South Florida. She has taught both graduate and undergraduate courses at USF during the past several years. Dr. Wooten is an interdisciplinary scientist in developing methematical and statistical models for GLOBAL WARMING, hurricanes, health sciences, volcanic studies and ecological processes, among others. She has an extensive list of publications in her area of research interests. Dr. Wooten is a popular lecturer and has made several research presentations. Her philanthropic services in mathematical sciences extend to her being a tutor and academic advisor at the award winning Urban Scholars Outreach Program at USF for underprivileged students. She is also a co-founder of the educational Dr. A.N.V. Rao Gurukulam Program. She is a member of several professional societies and the author of several techinal publications that promote quality undergraduate teaching in mathematical sciences.

Abolfazl Saghafi, Ph.D.

Director of International Affairs

  • Email: asaghafi@mail.usf.edu

Dr. Abolfazl Saghafi is a doctoral candidate at the University of South Florida and a distinguished expert on entropy measures for Weibull parameters and k order Poisson models. Prior to joining USF, Dr. Saghafi earned his doctoral degree in Iran and was on faculty in the Iranian university system teaching undergraduate mathematics and statistics courses for five years. Dr. Saghafi's current work expands his previous methods to machine learning algorithms and high dimensional data. Dr. Saghafi's experience working across the globe on diverse problems serves as the foundation for his many duties coordinating IFNA's global efforts.

Doo Young Kim, M.A.

Director of Information Technologies

  • Email: dooyoungkim@mail.usf.edu

Doo Young Kim is a doctoral degree candidate in Statistics at the department of Mathematics and Statistics at the University of South Florida. His doctoral research is in statistical analysis and modeling of environmental and health problems. Mr. Kim’s doctoral research is under the supervision of Prof. Chris P. Tsokos. He received his B.A. in Business Administration from the Kyungwon University in South Korea, and his M.A. in Statistics from the Ball State University, Indiana. He has been teaching high school Mathematics for 15 years before coming to the United States and he received graduate teaching award of Mathematical Sciences at the Ball State University with his M.A. degree. He is currently teaching courses in statistical computing and probability as a graduate teaching associate. Mr. Kim has presented several papers at professional meetings and has submitted some of his results on the subjects of his interest.
